Interview Questions

Interviewed on 1/19/23 by Michael R. Elder


What is your background?

Bellarmine BA Business

UofL Associate of Arts in Paralegal Studies

59 years old

Do you consider yourself tech savvy? Or a novice user? 

Higher level novice

What does a typical day look like when booking an appointment? 

I try to call early to book early morning appointments when businesses first open to have more available options. If online or an app, I do it as soon as the need arises.

Do you own a PC? Smart phone? 

No, but I own an iPad and iPhone 13

Do you often interact with apps?

Yes, I often interact with apps daily such as banking, news, paying bills, and weather.

How often do you book appointments ahead of time? 

I book up to a year for a doctor appointment. My doctor has options for both online and calling to make appointment. I book up to six months for a dentist, and I have to call to make the appointment. I book

services around the home such as plumbing, yard work, and electricians up to two weeks, and I usually have to call. I book a haircut two months out, and I have to call.

What features do you look for in an appointment scheduler? 

I look for immediate notification of the appointment and reminder

How far out in a calendar would you like to see appointment availability? 

Depends for different services.

Dr. – 1 year and 2 months

Dental – 8 months

Haircut – 4 months

Massage – 3 months

Plumber – 3 weeks to a month

Would you like to be able to cancel appointments sooner than 24 hours in advance?

Yes, I would like to be able to cancel appointments sooner than 24 hours in advance.

Would you like to see a list of available providers?

Yes, I think it would be helpful to see a list of available providers.

How do you normally access appointment booking? Website? App?

I normally use websites and more frequent appointments I make the appointments using apps. I prefer to make appointments online or on an app by far, but some I call if there is no other option.

Do you often look for a feature that is hard to find?

No, the features are usually straight forward.

How would you describe your past experience with appointment scheduling apps?

I would say that apps make the process much easier than calling, faster appointment booking, and more private. It also gives more time to think and decide on an appointment time, so you don’t get put on

the spot.

What do you currently like about the product? Dislike?

I like that the appointments are in a grid style, easy to see all the appointment availability, and the reminders and immediate confirmation.

Would you like reminders sent to you? How many?

Yes, I like reminders and I think 1 is good.

When would you like the reminders sent to you? How would you like them sent you (ex. Text)?

I would prefer the reminder 2 days prior, so it gives me time to plan if there is an issue. I like texts to remind you of appointments and a question about confirmation.

Would you use an appointment scheduler app if there was one available for various appointments?

Yes, I would definitely use that over calling.

When do you think the latest you can cancel an appointment be without a penalty?

I think the latest you should be able to cancel an appointment without penalty should be 24 hours.

Do you think there should be any down time for the app such as use only during business hours?

No

What is one word that describes this appointment scheduler?

Helpful
