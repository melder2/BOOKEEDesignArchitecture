Sherry - Scenario 1

Sherry has noticed a common complaint amongst her patients lately – they’ve all needed to schedule follow-up appointments, or alter existing ones, but can only do so if it’s during normal business hours
when she’s able to look at her pen & paper planner. She tells her patients that she is switching over to a new, digital appointment scheduling application, called Bookee, that will allow them to view available
timeslots 24/7. After providing a brief overview, the patients are able to go home and schedule follow-ups at their leisure. Additionally, some patients find that last-minute emergencies come up in their
everyday lives and they have no choice but to cancel appointments. However, they despise the hospital’s cancellation policy - which charges a fee for any cancelled, previously scheduled appointment. Sherry
is able to calm her patients’ added anxiety by telling them that Bookee allows free cancellation for any appointment, up to 24 hours in advance.

Sherry - Scenario 2

Sherry has been approached by Larry, a new colleague, and recent graduate, in the psychology department of the hospital where she works and asked how she’s able to juggle so many patients back-to-back
throughout the day. Larry wants to make sure that he has enough of a gap between patient sessions tofinalize his notes and recollect himself before seeing the next patient. Previously, Larry used pen & paper
to keep track of his schedule but finds it too easy to accidentally cause overlap and conflicting appointments. Sherry helps Larry register to Bookee with a unique user ID and begin importing his current day’s
schedule. She learns that Larry would like a color-coding system that displays if a patient is a referral, established, or new. Sherry then shows him that Bookee allows each timeslot to be highlighted in three
different colors.

Sherry - Scenario 3

Sherry has been asked by Rodney, the head of the Psychology department in her hospital, to test pilot Bookee for managing her patient’s appointments. Rodney wants to make sure that healthcare providers
will be able to easily view their upcoming appointments in daily, weekly, and monthly views. Sherry begins by adding all of her day’s appointments to Bookee to give an overview of the work ahead. She
selects different timeslots to see the name of the patient scheduled and their most recent session notes. She asked colleagues if there were any features they’d like to see most in Bookee, and the consensus
was a “streamlined interface.”
