Sherry, age 59, is a psychologist for a large, local hospital. She specializes in early childhood development and attention-deficit disorders. She was born in Glasgow, KY where her father owned a chain of apartment complexes while her mother stayed home to raise their eight children. After earning numerous degrees in the field of medicine, with an emphasis in psychology, from the University of Kentucky, she moved to Louisville to be with her newlywed husband to start a family and begin treating patients. She has two children: a son aged 31 and a daughter, aged 35. Her son is a Software Engineer for a startup company, and her daughter is an insurance agent specializing in home coverage. Sherry likes to get home as early as possible from the office so that she can pick up her grandkids from school and spoil them with desserts before dinner. Sherry uses a popular calendar organization application to keep track of her patient’s appointments but sometimes finds the app to be overwhelming with all of its features and customizations. She does not consider herself “tech-savvy” and misses the days of “good ‘ol pen & paper” for keeping track of her patients’ schedules. She loathes the telemedicine environment and avoids its use if at all possible – she prefers face-to-face interactions with her patients because she believes “it builds trust.” She’s open to using Bookee for her primary appointment scheduling application if it was “user-friendly and simple.” However, she is not interested in viewing estimated parking costs or related features if offered.
